Acoustic metamaterials. The group studies materials that leverage subwavelength dynamics to achieve an exotic acoustic response. These so-called acoustic metamaterials find applications in a broad range of disciplines. Examples include acoustic cloaks, negative lenses, pentamode materials, non-reciprocal beams and surfaces, and Willis materials. For an overview of acoustic metamaterials, see this article from Acoustics Today.

Nondestructive evaluation. Nondestructive evaluation refers to the use of ultrasound to noninvasively monitor the structural health of materials. The group contributes to this area in theoretical and experimental capacities...

Additive manufacturing. In recent years, additive manufacturing has become a promising and reliable means to fabricate acoustic components. In addition to conducting fundamental research on the acoustic properties of additively manufactured materials, the group additively manufactures many of its own metamaterials.

Physical acoustics. The group is involved in the field of physical acoustics, the most fundamental investigation of sound, with contributions to the study of reflectors, acoustic radiation force and torque, and acoustic vortex beams.
